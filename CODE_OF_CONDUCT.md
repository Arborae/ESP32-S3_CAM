# Code of Conduct

Arborae is built on a respectful, collaborative and welcoming environment.
This applies to everyone taking part in this project — contributors,
maintainers and users alike.

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience
for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance,
race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Mutual respect and empathy toward other people
- Giving and gracefully accepting **constructive feedback**
- Focusing on what is best for the project and the community
- Showing courtesy in issues, pull requests and discussions

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, discrimination or personal attacks of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the maintainers privately — via a private
[Discussion](https://github.com/orgs/arborae/discussions) or by contacting a
maintainer directly on GitHub. Please do **not** open a public issue for a
code-of-conduct concern.

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards and will take appropriate,
fair action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, up to and
including a temporary or permanent ban from the project's spaces.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (repository, issues,
pull requests, discussions) and when an individual is representing the project
in public spaces.
